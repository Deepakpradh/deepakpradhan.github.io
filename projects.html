Active Matter Simulation

LAMMPS MD Simulation M.Sc. Project
2023-2024 | IIT Madras

Topic: Collective motion of cells modeled as ring polymers in active matter systems. This research explores how individual cellular components interact to produce emergent collective behavior.

Research Guide: Prof. P.B. Sunilkumar

Department of Physics, IIT Madras

Technologies & Tools Used:

LAMMPS
Python
Data Analysis
Molecular Dynamics

Key Outcomes:

  • Developed computational models for ring polymer dynamics in active environments
  • Implemented custom modifications to LAMMPS for specific interaction potentials
  • Analyzed collective motion patterns and phase transitions in active matter
  • Generated visualizations of polymer chain dynamics and aggregation behavior

Research Insights:

This project demonstrated how simple interaction rules at the microscopic level can lead to complex collective behavior, providing insights into biological processes like tissue formation and bacterial colony dynamics.

Quantum Computing Foundations

Quantum B.Sc. Project
2021-2022 | BJB College

Topic: Introduction to Quantum Computing principles and algorithms. This project explored the fundamental differences between classical and quantum computation, focusing on quantum gates, superposition, and entanglement.

Research Guide: Prof. Rajmohan Mohanty

Department of Physics, BJB Autonomous College

Technologies & Concepts:

Quantum Algorithms
Circuit Models
Qiskit
Linear Algebra

Key Outcomes:

  • Understanding of quantum superposition and entanglement principles
  • Implementation of basic quantum algorithms (Deutsch-Jozsa, Grover's)
  • Analysis of quantum computational advantages over classical approaches
  • Exploration of quantum error correction and decoherence challenges

Research Insights:

This foundational project highlighted the paradigm shift quantum computing represents, emphasizing how quantum mechanical principles could revolutionize computational problem-solving in fields from cryptography to material science.

Research Methodology

Systematic approach to computational physics research

Starting from physical principles to develop mathematically precise models that capture essential system behavior while remaining computationally tractable.

Simulation Implementation

Translating mathematical models into efficient code using appropriate computational tools (LAMMPS, Python) with careful attention to numerical stability.

Analysis & Visualization

Extracting meaningful insights from simulation data through statistical analysis and creating intuitive visualizations that reveal underlying patterns.
