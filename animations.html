Computational Mathematics: The Pursuit Problem

Where mathematics meets beautiful geometric patterns

The N-Bugs Problem

In this classic mathematical puzzle, n entities (snails/bugs) start at the vertices of a regular polygon. Each moves at constant speed toward its clockwise neighbor, creating beautiful logarithmic spirals and demonstrating differential equations in action.

dr/dt = v(cos θ - 1),   r dθ/dt = v sin θ

3 Snails (Triangle)

Three snails starting at triangle vertices. The pursuit creates equiangular spirals converging at the centroid.

Regular Triangle

4 Snails (Square)

Four snails starting at square vertices. Demonstrates how initial geometry affects the pursuit trajectory.

Complex Systems: Chaos Theory

Deterministic equations, unpredictable outcomes

The Lorenz Attractor

A system of three ordinary differential equations that exhibits chaotic behavior. Known for the "butterfly effect" - where small differences in initial conditions yield vastly different outcomes.

dx/dt = σ(y - x),   dy/dt = x(ρ - z) - y,   dz/dt = xy - βz

This animation visualizes the strange attractor in phase space, demonstrating how deterministic equations can produce complex, non-repeating patterns.

Lorenz Attractor Visualization

σ (Prandtl): 10
ρ (Rayleigh): 28
β (Dimension): 8/3
