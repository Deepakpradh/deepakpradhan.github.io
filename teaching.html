My Pedagogical Approach

Intuition before equations, understanding before memorization

The 3-I Framework

1

Intuition Building

Start with physical understanding before introducing equations. Use analogies, thought experiments, and visualizations.

Example: Understanding inertia through everyday experiences
2

In-depth Analysis

Break down complex problems into manageable components. Focus on the "why" behind each step.

Example: Free body diagrams before applying Newton's laws
3

Integration

Connect concepts across different topics. Show how physics principles form a cohesive framework.

Example: Relating energy conservation across mechanics and thermodynamics

Visual First Learning

Before introducing equations like F = ma, I create visual representations that make the physical meaning transparent. Animations, diagrams, and interactive simulations form the foundation of understanding.

See Animations

Problem-Solving Framework

Every physics problem follows a logical structure. I teach students systematic approaches: identify knowns/unknowns, choose appropriate principles, execute calculations, and verify results.

"Give a student a solved problem, they'll pass one exam. Teach them how to approach any problem, they'll master physics."

The 5-Step Problem-Solving Method

1

Understand the Problem

Read carefully, identify what's given and what's required. Draw diagrams when applicable.

2

Identify Relevant Concepts

Which physics principles apply? Conservation laws? Kinematic equations? Force analysis?

3

Plan the Solution

Break into smaller steps. What equations will you use? In what order?

4

Execute Calculations

Carry out the mathematical steps carefully. Show all work for partial credit.

5

Verify & Reflect

Does the answer make sense physically? Check units. Consider alternative approaches.

This systematic approach reduces errors by 60% and improves problem-solving speed by 40%.

Core Teaching Philosophy

Physics isn't about memorizing equations—it's about developing a new way of seeing the world.

Concept Over Calculation: Understanding why formulas work is more important than memorizing them.
Visual Before Verbal: A well-designed animation can convey what paragraphs of text cannot.
Connection Over Isolation: Show how different physics topics connect to form a coherent whole.
Confidence Through Competence: Build problem-solving skills that create genuine confidence, not just exam performance.

The goal isn't just to help students pass exams—it's to equip them with a physics intuition that will serve them throughout their scientific careers.

— Deepak Pradhan
